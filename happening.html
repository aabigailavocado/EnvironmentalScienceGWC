How Does Plastic Affect Us?

Environment

Because there are plastic debris in the oceans, smaller fragments of plastic can be swallowed by fish leading to an increase in fish deaths. Turtles can get stuck in fishing nets while smaller fish can get stuck in fishing lewers. Frequent plastic digestion can harm fish and other ocean species which directly impacts the food chain.


Climate Change

While plastics break down, they release greenhouse gases that travel up to the atmosphere which prevent heat from leaving the earth. Plastics also break down into methane and ethylene which add to the greenhouse gases. Millions of tons of plastic are constantly adding to greenhouse gases that trap heat and the remaining debris remains in the ocean. As the earth is getting hotter, more ice is melting at a rapid pace and many animals such as bears are losing their habitat.


Health

Ocean pollution can cause health issues such as respiratory problems, reproductive, developmental, and neurological disorders. Microplastics have been detected in the human body and pose a serious threat to bodily functions and cells.
